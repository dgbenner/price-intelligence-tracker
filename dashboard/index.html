What is Dollar Cost Averaging?

Dollar cost averaging means buying regularly at the average price over time, rather than trying to time the market for the absolute lowest price. By tracking prices and buying at the best average price point, you minimize cost while maintaining a consistent supply.

How TruePrice Beats the Algorithm

Major retailers use a playbook of pricing tactics designed to make you feel like you're getting a deal — even when you're not. Dynamic pricing adjusts costs hour by hour. Flash sales create artificial urgency. Inflated "was" prices make markdowns look deeper than they are. Subscribe-and-save discounts, limited-time coupons, and member-exclusive offers all serve one purpose: to obscure what a product actually costs so you'll buy on impulse instead of information.

TruePrice strips away that noise. By tracking real transaction prices across multiple retailers over weeks and months, we reveal the true typical value of every product we monitor. When you can see that a "40% off" sale is actually just this week's normal price at another store, you stop falling for the games. You see exactly what a product costs on average — and whether today's price is genuinely worth acting on. No more guessing, no more manufactured urgency — just clear data that puts you back in control of your spending.
